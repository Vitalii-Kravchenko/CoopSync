Reconnecting with a friend — from either side — no longer requires a detour through Leave and onboarding just to type a username.

## New

- 🤝 **Accept an invite right from Settings, not just onboarding** — whenever there's no working storage (nothing set up yet, or a host removed you), Settings used to offer only "Create storage." It now also shows the same one-click "X invited you" banner onboarding gets, plus a manual field to type a friend's username — so connecting to (or switching to) a friend no longer means leaving and going through onboarding again just to type a name.
- 🎉 **Onboarding now tells you when someone's already invited you** — previously the only way to accept an invite was to already know the exact username of whoever invited you. If a friend re-invited you after you left or were removed, you had no way to know unless you happened to type their name in again. Onboarding now checks for pending invites once you're logged in and shows "X invited you to their storage" with a one-click connect button.

## Fixes

- 🔄 **"Create storage" no longer leaves you half-connected** — creating storage from Settings' fallback button only touched GitHub, never updated who the app thinks you're connected to. The new repo existed, but the app kept trying to manage the old one — inviting anyone to it was silently rejected. Now sets that correctly, same as every other path that makes you a host.
- ⏳ **"Create storage" shows the same spinner as everywhere else** — it used to swap its own label to "Creating…" instead of the spinner used throughout the rest of the app. Fixed in both Settings and onboarding.
